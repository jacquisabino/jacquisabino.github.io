"Services" = the OpenAI website, applications and services
"The Privacy Policy" = Describes OpenAI practices with respect to personal data collected about users and how that data is handled.
"Personal Data" = Personal data relating to you. OpenAI has split this into three categaories of collected data. Personal data you provide, personal data OpenAI receives from use of OpenAI services, and information received from other sources.
"Account Information" = Info associated with OpenAI account. Name, contact info, account credentials, date of birth, payment info and transaction history. Additionally, profile picture, username, or other information provided to OpenAI Services (OpenAI API, OpenAI community forum, etc...)
User Content ("Content") = Personal Data the user provides in the input to OpenAI Services.
As I am writing this, I have yet to finish reading the entire policy. I am however grateful for the email sent to me regarding the updated privacy policy. As this allowed me to delete one account and keep the other at the risk of personal information being shared. OpenAI explicilty states that they collect Personal Data if an account is created for their use of services or to communciate with OpenAI and its' affilicates. Does this mean I should feel safer using it sans account? I don't know yet.
It is unclear to me if using a ChatGPT integrated tool, such as Booking.com's AI trip planner counts as and OpenAI service and if the use of those tools can be opted out of. Afterall, we are simply users of the open internet. Additionally, are there any ChatGPT integrated search englines or models for companies whose integration is not as clear? For example, Estee Laudre Companies use generative AI services in their customer service interactions. The details are laid out in a current, paywalled, Vogue article. But I can make an educated guess that this means voice models for a phone line and a text assistant on their website. There is an added layer of obfuscation when you consider that OpenAI's privacy policy does not apply to content processed on behalf of these business deals. Basically, Booking.com and Estee Laudre have their own agreements with ChatGPT specifying use of data. One is inclined to believe they have more incentive to protect their own data such as financial reports, clinical trials, or even future business endeavors as opposed to consumer collected data with no forseeable profit. Does Estee Laudre care about our questions to its' helpline? If so, that conversation will be stored for their future benefit. If not, I have to assume its' given to OpenAI for enhancement of their models.
It should come as no shock that OpenAI has created an abundance of loopholes to collect personal data. Particularly, from third parties. But, I'm an optimist. OpenAI has divided the category of personal data collected into three subcategories. Personal Data the user provides. Personal Data OpenAI receives from a users' use of OpenAI services. Information OpenAI receives from other sources. The most interesting of which being the latter.
The first subcategory is pretty self-explanatory. This would be your account information, prompts to ChatGPT, messages sent in the OpenAI community forum, and data used for the personalization and enhanced experience of third-party apps such as Canva, Coursera, Adobe Photoshop, and Grammarly. This is actually my first time learning about 'ChatGPT apps'. They are basically multiple AI-assistants that give you seamless exepriences for a data trade. Communication Information is basic, when you communicate with OpenAI via email or social media, they can collect your name, phone number, email, anything needed to contact you, and contents of those messages. I actually find it pretty funny thinking of hundreds of Twitter and Instagram handles associated with "Your the devil, die", or "Why no porn?". Is there any use? Isolated, no. But I can definately see advantages to adding this little bit of character defining data to a user profile that likely cosists of personally identifying data such as IP address, hometown, language spoken, schools attended, preferred names, emails, etc... And once you've reached this stage, you can easily start mapping to friends and family. That part, however, is not so clear.
