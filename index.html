"The Privacy Policy" = Describes OpenAI practices with respect to personal data collected about users and how that data is handled. "Personal Data" = Personal data relating to you. OpenAI has split this into three categaories of collected data. Personal data you provide, personal data OpenAI receives from use of OpenAI services, and information received from other sources.
As I am writing this, I have yet to finish reading the entire policy. I am however grateful for the email sent to me regarding the updated privacy policy. As this allowed me to delete one account and keep the other at the risk of personal information being shared. They explicilty state that they collect Personal Data if an account is created for their use of services or to communciate with OpenAI and its' affilicates. Does this mean I should feel safer using it sans account? I don't know yet. It is unclear to me if using a ChatGPT integrated tool, such as Booking.com's AI trip planner counts as and OpenAI service and if the use of those tools can be opted out of. Afterall, we are simply users of internet sites such as Booking.com. Additionally, are there any ChatGPT integrated search englines or models for companies whose integration is not as clear? For example, Estee Laudre Companies using generative AI services in their customer service interactions. The details are laid out in a current, paywalled Vogue article. But I can make an educated guess that this means voice models for a phone line and a text assistant on their website. There is an added layer of obfuscation when you consider that OpenAI's privacy policy does not apply to content processed on behalf of these business deals. Both Booking.com and Estee Laudre have their own agreements with ChatGPT specifying use of data. One is inclined to believe they have more incentive to protect their own data such as financial reports, clinical trials, or even future business endeavors as opposed to consumer collected data with no forseeable profit. Does Estee Laudre care about our questions to its' helpline? If so, that conversation will be stored for their future benefit. If not, I have to assume its' given to OpenAI for enhancement of their models. It should come as no shock that OpenAI has created an abundance of loopholes to collect personal data related to individuals. Particularly, from third parties. But on the bright side, it seems that your data cannot be collected without the creation of an account.
